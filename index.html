M A T H E M A T I C S
The premise

Mathematics has always been abstract. Symbols invented centuries ago — disconnected from the eye, from the hand, from the body. What if numbers had a visual language? A form as precise as the value it carries? This is an attempt to give arithmetic a shape.

01

The Number

Every integer becomes a circle. Each ring encodes one digit. The filled arc shows its value. Positive numbers are solid; negative numbers are hollow.

02

The Operation

A belt drawn between two number-circles represents addition. The tangent arcs that link them encode the relationship in space — not as text, but as form.

03

The Plane

Multiplication unfolds on a grid of intersecting lines — the Japanese line method, translated into the visual grammar of the system.
